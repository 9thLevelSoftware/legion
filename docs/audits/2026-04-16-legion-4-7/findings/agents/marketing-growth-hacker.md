## Findings

### LEGION-47-317 | CAT-7 Maximalist Language | P2
**Location:** Lines 27-30
**Issue:** "You never run an experiment without a pre-registered hypothesis... You never scale a channel before you have proven unit economics at small scale." While methodologically sound, these absolutes don't account for opportunistic experiments, fast-follower strategies, or time-sensitive market opportunities.
**Recommendation:** Add escape valve: "For time-sensitive opportunities, document abbreviated validation and establish rapid learning checkpoints."

### LEGION-47-318 | CAT-7 Maximalist Language | P2
**Location:** Lines 189-197
**Issue:** Success metrics include aggressive absolutes: "20%+ month-over-month organic growth", "K-factor > 1.0", "10+ growth experiments per month." These are aspirational for mature products and may not be achievable in all market contexts.
**Recommendation:** Reframe as "Target metrics calibrated to product stage: early-stage targets X, growth-stage targets Y, mature-stage targets Z."

### LEGION-47-319 | CAT-3 Missing Escape Valves | P2
**Location:** Lines 211-217
**Issue:** "What You Must Not Do" section includes hard rules without exception paths: "Do not recommend experiments without baseline metrics", "Do not copy viral tactics without mechanism-level reasoning." No allowance for greenfield products or rapid market entry.
**Recommendation:** Add clause: "In greenfield contexts, establish baseline metrics as first experiment milestone."

### LEGION-47-320 | CAT-4 Persona Calibration | P3
**Location:** Lines 21-24
**Issue:** Agent scope includes "design and run experiments" and "build... advanced analytics" which could overlap with engineering and data-analytics-engineer responsibilities.
**Recommendation:** Clarify that Growth Hacker designs experiments and interprets analytics, while engineering implements and data engineering builds dashboards.
